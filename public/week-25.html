This week we dive into Jeremiah 4-29!
Hey Reading Community,

By now, we are all hopefully a little wiser having gone through the Hebrew Wisdom Literature. If only the nation of Israel had adopted some of this wisdom, perhaps they could have avoided exile, but that, unfortunately, is not how it went down.

This week, we are introduced to the first half of a prophet's work who was alive to witness the exile itself, meet Jeremiah. He was appointed by God as a prophet to Israel and the Nations, and like many prophets had both a message of judgment and hope. He informed the Hebrew people that God would punish them for their idolatry and neglect of the most vulnerable in society, but they would not listen (ch 1-24). Your reading this week will culminate in Jeremiah's confrontation of Israel with a message of judgment (ch 25), and especially with Israel's leadership (chs. 26-29).

Next week, you will begin reading about the siege and destruction of Jerusalem, including the temple, from an eye witness account of Jeremiah himself. These are some of the most sorrowful writings you will read in the Old Testament. 

Onwards!

Jon, Tim, & The Bible Project Crew

Week 25
Summary

This week, we are introduced to the work of the Prophet Jeremiah. This book will read a little like an anthology because that's what it is. It is a collection of sermons, poems, and essays from over 20 years of Jeremiah's priesthood (see ch. 36).

Our reading focuses on the first half of Jeremiah (through ch. 29). Jeremiah was alive for the decades leading up to the siege of Jerusalem, and its eventual fall and destruction of the temple. He was sent as a prophet by God to Judah to warn them of the grave consequences their covenant unfaithfulness would bring—namely through the nation of Babylon.

This covenant unfaithfulness is what Jeremiah focuses on in the first half of the book, including the worship of other Gods, rampant social injustice, and even the grotesque practice of child sacrifice. Jeremiah's messages are all wrapped up in very eloquent fashion in Jeremiah's temple sermon in ch. 7—we highly recommend you read the blog this week. However, the people do not receive Jeremiah's message, and so in ch. 25 Jeremiah announces the certainty of God's judgment, which would be brought against Israel and the other nations by Babylon. 

Next week, we will read the rest of Jeremiah's description of God's judgment through Babylon, as well as his central message of hope in chs. 30-33.
