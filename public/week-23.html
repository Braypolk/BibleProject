This week we dive into Proverbs 7-27!
Hey Reading Community,

You’re already into the book of Proverbs at this point. But have you noticed, that the classic, short proverbs, don’t start until chapter 10? Why does the book start with nine chapters of long speeches? This is all by design, as these speeches introduce you to the book as a whole and how to read it. We highly encourage you to read this week’s blog and watch both videos on Proverbs to help you grasp what is going on in this timeless book; the proverbs themselves are only half of the story!

The collections of proverbs you find in chapters 10 through 29 are not intended to be read just once but over the course of a lifetime. These short sayings cover the range of human experiences at different stages of life, and they’re designed to guide you towards a virtuous life. It's important to keep coming back to proverbs at all stages of your life, whether you’re a child or parent, husband or wife, boss, or employee. The proverbs are for all people, helping you see God’s wisdom in the order of his world, and the wisdom in how to properly interact with it.

Onwards!

Jon, Tim, & The Bible Project Crew

Week 23
Summary
The book of Proverbs has a clear literary design and structure and is not just an aggregated list of short, clever sayings. Proverbs 1:1-9 very explicitly informs the reader that this book is profitable for gaining wisdom, which is an expressly Hebrew concept of “applied knowledge,” its foundation being a healthy fear of the Lord.

In chapters 1-9, you’ll find ten speeches given from a father to a son that create the framework for the rest of the book. You could call it the “moral logic” of the book of Proverbs. You’ll also find four distinct poems by a character referred to as “Lady Wisdom,” which clues us into the divine and human nature of wisdom literature. Proverbs are not merely good advice, but God’s personal invitation to learn wisdom from previous generations and tap into an invisible attribute of God that he wants to share with us.

In chapters 10-29, you will find hundreds of these proverbs that cover the gamut of topics, all filtered through the framework of chapters 1-9. These are not formulas for how to get ahead in life, but simply generally wise ways of living in relatively normal circumstances. However, exceptions to proverbs are a dime a dozen, which is why there are two additional books of wisdom literature to guide the reader through these situations. As you close the book of Proverbs, you’ll find two examples (chs. 30 and 31) of how someone might use this book and its proverbs to practically live out a more wise, virtuous, and God-pleasing life.
