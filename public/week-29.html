This week we finish Ezekiel and start Ezra and Nehemiah!
Hey Reading Community,

We hope reading Ezekiel has been a powerful and insightful experience. This week, you will wrap up the book finishing the final temple vision. In chapters 40-48, Ezekiel has a vision of a new garden temple bringing new life to all of creation, consistent with other themes Ezekiel began in other chapters. This leaves you on a high note, just in time for the long-awaited return from Exile.

Ezra-Nehemiah was originally one scroll and was intended to be told as a unified story. It retells the story of the first waves of Israelites to return to Jerusalem by focusing on three key leaders. However, this is also one of the most overlooked books in the Old Testament and is often misunderstood. Read this week's blog to hear more about these common misconceptions.

Onwards!

Week 29
Summary
The unified Ezra-Nehemiah story tells a realistic story of religious people who are zealous to help others see the world and God, and do everything in their power to lead the Israelite's into a new era of devotion to their God. Sadly, this hope falls flat and the story ends with the Israelites violating the covenant commands yet again, and the leader's inability to create the change in the people that was so desperately needed.

The story is told as three parallel movements, but this week we will focus on the first two for the reading. Ezra 1-6 covers Zerubbabel and Joshua who lead the first wave of exiles back from Babylon. Ezra 7-10 specifically covers a spiritual revival movement among the exiles, but both end with lackluster results. Next week, the story will culminate and show how it fits into the larger story, and why it is essential to the biblical narrative.
